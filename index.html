Visualizing

top Football Clubs

A comparison of the skills, talent, and valuations of football players in seven top clubs - Real Madrid, FC Barcelona, Juventus, Paris Saint-Germain, Manchester United, Manchester City, FC Bayern München.

Dataset

I am using the Fifa 19 complete player dataset extracted from the latest FIFA database. The dataset lists detailed player attributes like biographical information, skill levels, and valuations. This data was preprocessed and manipulated using Google Sheets before being fed to Tableau for visualizations.

Dataset Source!

Question 1 : Judging Talent

Which teams have more talented players? In terms of the overall rating of the players, what is the distribution of the players? Considering a threshold of 85 to identify top talent, how many top talent players are present in each team?

Visualization 1: Judging Talent



From this box-and-whisker plot, it is clear that Juventus has a greater number of higher overall rated players. The other teams have more or less the same median overall player rating. Manchester City and FC Bayern München have multiple players who are rated below 60. The two extreme outliers for Juventus and FC Barcelona are Cristiano Ronaldo and Lionel Messi respectively.




This treemaps plot shows the number of top talent players in each of the 7 clubs. Real Madrid comes out on top, thanks to their Galácticos policy. Paris Saint-Germain and Manchester United have very few top talent players while the remaining clubs are close to Real Madrid's count.

Question 2 : Gauging Skill

Beyond just overall rating, a player's skill level in the position they play is another metric that begs comparison across different clubs. Which clubs outperform others in these areas?

Visualization 2: Gauging Skills


For each area of the pitch, 5 defining traits have been defined. A radial (spider) chart based on the (normalized) average scores of these traits, for players who play in these areas, is plotted for each area.




The 5 defining traits for a player in the forward position are - Shot Power, Acceleration, Agility, Finishing, and Jumping. These traits are varied across different clubs and as such, each club uses their distinct tactics to score goals. The highest variation in a particular trait is for Jumping.




The 5 defining traits for a player in the midfield position are - Short Passing, Balance, Ball Control, Dribbling, and Positioning. Juventus outperforms all other clubs in the majority of these traits. The highest variation in a particular trait is for Positioning.




The 5 defining traits for a player in the defense position are - Standing Tackle, Header Accuracy, Interceptions, Marking, and Sliding Tackle. Juventus again outperforms all other clubs in the majority of these traits and that too by a huge margin. Although not obvious from a static image, in an interactive visualization one can see that Paris Saint-Germain has the poorest defense.




The 5 defining traits for a player in the goalkeeping position are - Reflexes, Diving, Handling, Kicking, and Positioning. Manchester United has the most well-rounded goalkeepers, whereas Manchester City needs to improve all these traits in its goalkeepers. There is quite a high amount of variance across all traits.

Question 3 : Assessing Valuations

Given these skills and perceived talent, how are the players of these clubs evaluated in the transfer market? Does the position of a player affect their valuation? How does the evaluation vary according to age/experience?

Visualization 3: Assessing Valuations


For each position of the pitch (forward, midfield, defense, goalkeeping) the valuations of each player are plotted as a scaled dot scatter plot. The color of the dot is indicative of the cost of the valuation. The size of the dot is indicative of the overall rating of the player. Generally, the higher the valuation the greater the overall rating.




Forward players have the highest valuation - to win matches you need to score goals. The extreme outliers in FC Barcelona and Paris Saint-Germain are Lionel Messi and Kylian Mbappe respectively. There aren't enough forward players in FC Bayern München indicating that they rely on their offensive midfielders to score goals.




Midfield players earn considerably less than their forward counterparts. The extreme outlier in Manchester City is Kevin De Bruyne. Juventus has the strongest midfield yet none of their players earn over 50M.




Defense players earn even less than midfield players. Paris Saint-Germain should invest in better players to improve their defense. FC Bayern München has an expensive defense lineup suggesting that they play more defensively




Goalkeepers earn more or less the same as defensive players. Manchester United has the most expensive goalkeeper - which is why their Goalkeeping skills are the best among the 7 clubs.




Here the size and color of the dot indicate the valuation of the player. As a general trend, valuation increases with age and higher rated players are valued more. However, the overall rating (and consequently their valuation) tends to decrease after a certain age (26) as indicated by the falling red circles. However, two outliers do exist - Lionel Messi and Cristiano Ronaldo are still rated close to 93 despite being 31 years old - truly, a remarkable achievement. On the other side of the spectrum, Kylian Mbappe - the red dot at age 19, is the most promising top talent of the future.
